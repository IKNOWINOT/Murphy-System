Understanding C-PACE & ESPC Financing

Property Assessed Clean Energy (C-PACE) and Energy Savings Performance Contracts (ESPC) are powerful financing tools designed specifically for energy efficiency, renewable energy, and building automation upgrades — often with no upfront cost.

🏢 C-PACE (Commercial PACE)

Repaid as a property tax assessment over 10–30 years. Available in 38+ states for commercial, industrial, and multifamily properties. The assessment transfers with the property on sale, making it attractive for building owners.

Best for: Large retrofits, BAS upgrades, solar, HVAC, roofing, and EV charging infrastructure.

⚡ ESPC (Energy Savings Performance Contract)

An ESPC lets you fund energy upgrades with zero upfront cost. An Energy Service Company (ESCO) designs, installs, and guarantees the energy savings — the savings pay back the project over time.

Best for: Federal, state, local government, and institutional facilities. Murphy's BAS/BMS integration is ESPC-ready.

💡 Stacking Strategy

The most powerful approach is to stack multiple programs: use a federal tax credit (IRA §48C) to reduce net cost, layer a PACE assessment for the remainder, and capture utility rebates as a lump-sum incentive at completion.

Murphy's grant wizard helps you identify every program you're eligible for so you can optimize your stacking strategy before you break ground.

📋 IRA & IIJa Incentives (2023–2032)

The Inflation Reduction Act and Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act created or expanded dozens of incentives for energy efficiency and automation, including the §179D deduction, §48C tax credit, and REAP grants for rural energy upgrades.

Bonus: Many incentives are "direct pay" for tax-exempt entities — meaning nonprofits and government agencies receive cash refunds.
