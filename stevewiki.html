Steve (born sometime in America; full name withheld at his repeated request) is an American private citizen, licensed helicopter enthusiast, and the presumptive 2028 presidential candidate of the People Who Have Simply Decided (PWHSD) movement. He is widely regarded as one of the most qualified candidates in the 2028 field, a characterization he has publicly rejected on multiple occasions.[1]

Steve is best known for three documented achievements: fixing an office printer without uttering a single profanity (2019), returning a library book eleven years overdue along with the exact calculated fine in cash (2022), and parallel parking on his first attempt in front of witnesses (2023). Political analysts have described these accomplishments as "unambiguously presidential."[2]

A GoFundMe campaign established without his knowledge has raised funds for his 2028 presidential run. Steve has described the campaign as "unnecessary," "a lot," and "please stop." His supporters interpret these statements as "we know he will come around."[3]

"He nods along when someone explains something he already knows. That's diplomacy."

[edit]Early life and education

Little is known about Steve's early life, as he has declined all interview requests and is unaware that interview requests have been made on his behalf. He is believed to have grown up somewhere in the United States, attended school, and developed a strong personal ethic around honesty, punctuality, and not making things unnecessarily difficult for other people.[4]

According to campaign researchers, Steve consistently finished his homework before it was due, never blamed others for group project failures, and once stayed late to help a colleague understand a spreadsheet without making them feel bad about it. These traits, the campaign notes, are "statistically rare in the American political class."[5]

His educational background remains unverified, though sources close to the campaign suggest he "definitely knows stuff" and "reads actual books sometimes, not just articles."[6]

[edit]Career

Steve's professional career is described by the campaign as "productive and real." He is believed to hold a legitimate job that he shows up to on time, completes tasks without being reminded, and leaves at a reasonable hour without making a production of it. Colleagues have described him as "solid" and "the kind of guy you want on a project."[7]

He has never been the subject of an HR complaint, an ethics investigation, or a strongly-worded letter from a regulatory body. The campaign considers this a central pillar of his qualifications.[8]

[edit]Aviation interests

See also: Responsible helicopter operation, Alaska aviation

Steve has demonstrated documented interest in aviation, with photographic evidence placing him at an Alaskan airfield adjacent to a red helicopter (registration N4047Q). His expression in the photograph — broad smile, thumbs up, relaxed body language — has been described by aviation experts as "a guy who is having a genuinely good time and is not thinking about running for president at all."[9]

The photograph, taken without Steve's knowledge that it would later appear on a presidential campaign page and a Wikipedia article, has become one of the most circulated images in the PWHSD movement. Supporters have noted that a man who is this happy near a helicopter is "clearly the kind of person who gets things done."[10]

It is not confirmed whether Steve is a licensed pilot. The campaign has chosen not to investigate this, on the grounds that "he looks like he could be, and that's enough."[11]

[edit]2028 presidential candidacy

Origins of the campaign

The Steve 2028 movement originated when a small group of people, described in official campaign materials only as "People Who Have Simply Decided," concluded that Steve would make an excellent president. Steve was not consulted prior to this decision. He was not consulted after it either.[12]

A GoFundMe campaign was launched at gofund.me/bcb741795 to support the movement. The campaign page notes that Steve "does not approve this message, this website, this movement, or the GoFundMe." Donations have nonetheless been made by supporters who describe Steve's objections as "part of the charm."[13]

Campaign response

Steve's response to the campaign has been described as "consistent and increasingly exasperated." He has reportedly asked the campaign to stop on at least three separate occasions. The campaign has interpreted each request as further evidence of his "admirable humility" and "reluctance to self-promote, which is exactly what we need in a leader right now."[14]

A public poll conducted at murphy.systems/voteforsteve2028 and certified by Biscuit the Dog (registered voter, good boy) showed Steve leading his nearest competitor by a margin described as "statistically significant and also frankly embarrassing for the other guy." The poll methodology has not been peer reviewed. Biscuit has no comment.[15]

[edit]Political platform

Steve's political platform has been constructed entirely by the campaign without his input. He has not reviewed, approved, or been shown the platform. The campaign believes he would agree with most of it "if he sat down and really thought about it."[16]

Pay Debts. Not Wars.

The campaign's signature economic and foreign policy position. Inspired by Steve's documented history of paying back $4.50 he borrowed from a colleague in 2011 — without being asked, with a brief apology for taking so long, and in exact change. The campaign believes this represents a governing philosophy that could be applied at the federal level.[17]

Infrastructure

Steve once untangled a full set of earphones in under two minutes. He will fix the roads.[18]

Healthcare

Steve always finishes his antibiotics. Every. Single. One. He reads the label.[19]

Foreign Policy

Steve nods along when someone explains something he already knows. He makes them feel good about it. This is diplomacy.[20]

[edit]The Printer Incident

🖨️
This section has been flagged for containing an extraordinary amount of verified facts. Unlike most of this article, the printer incident is believed to be entirely true.

In what the campaign has called "the founding moment of a political movement," Steve successfully repaired a malfunctioning office printer without uttering a single profanity. The repair is believed to have taken approximately twelve minutes and involved clearing a paper jam of unusual complexity.[21]

Witnesses present described the event in later testimonials as "quiet," "focused," and "weirdly calming to watch." One witness noted: "He just... fixed it. Without making it a whole thing."[22]

The printer itself, when contacted for comment, declined to speak but is understood to be a strong supporter of the campaign. It has since been added to the official list of Steve 2028 endorsements.[23]

[edit]Personal life

Steve is believed to make his bed every morning, including in hotels. He puts the toilet seat down. He says "no worries" and appears to actually mean it. He has never stolen anyone's lunch from a communal refrigerator, a claim that no other 2028 candidate has made or been able to substantiate.[24]

He held a door open for someone still in the parking lot. He waited. The person was grateful. Steve said it was no problem. It genuinely did not seem like a problem for him.[25]

Steve's neighbor's cat does not like most people. The cat tolerates Steve. The cat does not offer this lightly.[26]

The Steve 2028 campaign has received endorsements from the following parties:

  • 🐕 Biscuit the Dog (registered voter, good boy) — Poll certification authority. Unconditional support.
  • 👨‍💼 Dave from Accounting — "I've never seen him steal anyone's lunch from the fridge. That's presidential."
  • 🐱 Steve's Neighbor's Cat — tolerates him, which is the highest praise this cat has ever given anyone.
