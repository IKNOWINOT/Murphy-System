# Executive Brief: 50,000 sqft Mixed-Use Building HVAC Retrofit Bid Evaluation
## Introduction
Our MEP engineering firm is considering bidding on a 50,000 sqft mixed-use building HVAC retrofit project. This brief summarizes the project scope, identifies key risks, and provides a go/no-go recommendation.

## Scope Summary
The project involves:
* Replacing the existing HVAC system with a new, high-efficiency system
* Upgrading the building automation system (BAS) for improved control and monitoring
* Integrating the new HVAC system with the existing plumbing and electrical systems
* Ensuring compliance with current building codes and regulations
* Completing the retrofit within a 12-week construction schedule

## Risk Register
The following risks have been identified:
* **Risk 1: Unknown Existing Conditions**: Potential for hidden or unforeseen conditions in the existing building systems, which could impact the project schedule and budget.
* **Risk 2: Coordination with Other Trades**: Difficulty in coordinating with other contractors, such as plumbers and electricians, which could lead to delays or conflicts.
* **Risk 3: Permitting and Code Compliance**: Challenges in obtaining necessary permits and ensuring compliance with current building codes and regulations, which could result in project delays or fines.
* **Risk 4: System Integration**: Complexity in integrating the new HVAC system with the existing systems, which could lead to system performance issues or downtime.
* **Risk 5: Budget and Schedule Constraints**: Tight budget and schedule constraints, which could limit our ability to deliver the project on time and within budget.

## Go/No-Go Recommendation
Based on the scope summary and risk register, we recommend **proceeding with the bid**. The project aligns with our firm's expertise and experience in HVAC retrofits, and the potential rewards outweigh the identified risks. However, we must carefully manage the risks through:
* Thorough site investigations and surveys to identify potential existing conditions
* Close coordination with other contractors and stakeholders
* Proactive permitting and code compliance efforts
* Detailed system integration planning and testing
* Regular budget and schedule monitoring to ensure project constraints are met

By carefully managing these risks, we can deliver a successful project and establish a strong reputation in the mixed-use building market.
