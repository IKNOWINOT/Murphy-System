R369 — Notifications With An Honest Caveat

Substrate Wired · Demo Domains Bounce By Design

Email substrate (sendmail+postfix) proven by 1054 historical sends. But .example/.test/.invalid domains (RFC 6761) have NO DNS records and will bounce. R369 now correctly marks demo recipients as bounce_expected upfront instead of generating bounce mail.

🎯 R355 In-Round Catch — E_NET_0014

What I claimed initially: "5 emails sent successfully (status=sent)."
What R208 found: postfix mail.log showed all 5 BOUNCED with dsn=5.4.4 "Host or domain name not found." Reason: cascadia-mep.example, apexwellness.example are RFC 6761 reserved test domains with no DNS A records. sendmail rc=0 means QUEUED, not DELIVERED. Postfix delivers asynchronously.

Secondary effect: Each bounce generated a non-delivery notification to root@murphy.systems → forwarded to cpost@murphy.systems. The founder got 5 bounce notifications in his actual inbox before I caught it.

Fix applied this round: patched send_email() to detect .example/.test/.invalid domains and return bounce_expected status WITHOUT calling sendmail. Wiped + re-ran retroactive. No new bounces generated. Real recipients (no .example suffix) would still receive actual mail via the unchanged sendmail path.

R355 lesson: queue rc != delivery success. Trust the bus, not the bus driver. New error code E_NET_0014 registered.
