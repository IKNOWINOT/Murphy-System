The typical take rate for marketplaces ranges from 10% to 20%, so an 18% take rate is on the higher end. In contrast, a DTC model would likely have a much lower take rate, potentially around 2-5%, depending on payment processing fees and other expenses. However, DTC models often require significant investments in marketing, customer acquisition, and retention, which can impact profitability.

To calculate customer LTV, you'll need to consider factors such as average order value (AOV), purchase frequency, customer lifespan, and gross margin. A common formula for LTV is: LTV = (AOV x purchase frequency x customer lifespan) x gross margin. If your AOV is $50, purchase frequency is 5 times per year, customer lifespan is 3 years, and gross margin is 30%, your LTV would be approximately $225. However, if you're paying an 18% take rate to the marketplace, your net revenue per order would be lower, which could impact your LTV calculation.
