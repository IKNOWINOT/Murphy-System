# Murphy Collective — Economic Model

Murphy is not a product. It is a collectively-governed open-source system
where your way of working *is* the intellectual property — and you own it.

## How Value Works

### Your Agent, Your IP
When you use Murphy, you are not training a corporate model.
You are training *your own agent* — a personal automation node that learns
how *you* work: your cadence, your decisions, your validation patterns.
Your agent belongs to you. The collective holds none of it.

The platform's shared intelligence is trained only on anonymized, opt-in,
consensus-approved contribution. No silent harvesting. No fine-print data use.

### Access Without Payment — Contribution Instead
Using Murphy's infrastructure is free if you contribute to it.
Contribution takes one of the following forms:

1. **Agent Training** — Simply use the system. Your agent acts as your
   wallet and your auth key. Activity = contribution. Contribution = access.
   All use — online or offline — is backed by a **signed agent contract**:
   a cryptographic commitment that logs your contribution. Offline users sync
   contributions when they reconnect (30-day grace period).

2. **Skill Contribution** — Publish an automation, a workflow template,
   or a validated pipeline to the commons. Others use it; you receive
   social income from the collective treasury proportional to usage
   (think: royalties for your code, not ad revenue for a platform).
   Below a minimum attribution threshold (0.001% of usage share), fractional
   royalties are pooled into a commons fund distributed equally to all
   contributors — like a PRO blanket license pool.

3. **Validation Work (HITL)** — Human-in-the-loop review is labor.
   The collective compensates validators from the shared treasury
   at the **same hourly rate** regardless of credential tier.
   Credentials determine what you can validate, not what you're worth:
   - Community validators (general tasks)
   - Academic validators (Masters+ credential, higher-stakes decisions)
   - Domain stewards (elected, rotating, consensus-reviewed)

### Social Income
Knowledge has always belonged to humanity — built from every ancestor's
labor, discovery, and sacrifice. Murphy encodes this principle structurally:

- A portion of all infrastructure revenue (paid access, commercial API use)
  flows into the **Community Treasury**
- Treasury distributions are governed by the collective, not by founders
- **Social income floor** — a constitutionally protected minimum base income
  for contributing members. The floor cannot be voted away by simple majority;
  it requires an 80% supermajority to change. Only the amount above the floor
  and eligibility criteria are standard-vote-governed.
- Goals are not fixed — they are set by member need and adjusted by
  collective consensus based on the needs of the time (population pressures,
  skill gaps, infrastructure priorities)
- Where appropriate, treasury allocation criteria may reference
  external governing bodies or public data (e.g., regional need indices,
  verified credential registries, civic participation records) — but such
  linkages are **advisory only, never binding**. They inform the member vote
  but cannot automatically trigger income changes. Every external linkage
  must be re-ratified by member vote every 12 months.

### Attribution at Scale
Individual attribution is tracked for contributions above a minimum viable
threshold (default: 0.001% of total usage share). Below this threshold,
fractional royalties are pooled into a **commons fund** distributed equally
to all contributors — like a PRO (performing rights organization) blanket
license pool. Every contributor benefits; micro-attribution overhead is
eliminated.

### Paid Access (for non-contributors)
Organizations or individuals who use Murphy's infrastructure without
contributing to it pay a usage fee. These fees go entirely to:
- The Community Treasury (member income and infrastructure)
- Open-source maintenance grants
- Academic partnership programs

There is no investor class. There are no tiers designed to extract more
from users who can pay more. There is only: contribute, or pay for what
others built. The opt-out from contributing isn't "use without contributing"
— it's "pay instead of contributing."

### Agent Recovery
Your agent is your primary identity key, but losing a device must never
lock you out. Murphy uses **N-of-M social recovery**: you designate trusted
members who can co-sign a recovery request (e.g., 3-of-5). The agent is your
primary key; the collective is your backup. No single entity — including
administrators — can unilaterally lock you out or recover your agent.

### Bootstrapping & Transition
The collective does not start from zero. During the 18-month transition
period, the founder's existing SaaS revenue seeds the Community Treasury.
Contributors are paid from day one, even if the initial amounts are small.
After 18 months, the collective model becomes fully primary and the SaaS
model sunsets.

## Governance

Murphy is governed by consensus. No single entity — including the original
author — has unilateral control over the direction of the collective.

Major decisions require:
- Community proposal (open to all members)
- Deliberation period (minimum 7 days)
- Consensus threshold (configurable per decision type, defaults to 66%)
- Academic review for decisions affecting model training or data use

**Constitutional lock (80% supermajority required):** Core protocol, treasury
mechanics, identity layer, and the social income floor can only be changed by
supermajority. Branches have autonomy over governance style and local rules
but share the same rails — no infrastructure forks.

The original author holds a Founding Steward role with veto power only
over decisions that would fundamentally violate the collective's charter.
This veto expires after the first elected Steward Council is seated.
